USA

Why Choose USA

The United States universities are pioneers of top quality education and are known for their technical innovation. With over 3600 institutions, US provides the world's best education with an excellent and large variety of programs in both traditional courses as well as professional courses. A US degree is well sought after and is recognized worldwide. The education you will receive there will provide you with the needed understanding of every concept, the right attitude, and skills to be effective professionals and leaders in the international, interdependent and diverse society also it will make you an important part of world economy. Your educational stay in the US, apart from giving you academic excellence, will also enhance your independent thinking, cross-cultural communication skills, and self-confidence. The academic system provides flexibility to the students by offering not only a variety of programs but also giving an opportunity to design their own majors and programs of study.


Education System


Community Colleges in the United States offer a two-year degree which will not be recognized in Europe. However, transfer credits for courses will be accepted by an undergraduate program at a high-quality 4-year university if you have the sufficient eligibility requirements. A Community College is a low-cost and popular alternative for freshmen who seek to choose a less expensive option where they can gain their general education credit requirements. Almost half of the students in America start freshmen year in community colleges all over the country.

Colleges in the US offer work-related undergraduate degrees programs as well. Generally, the first two years include what is called general education and at some universities, it is often possible to start in the second year with a graduation diploma. Colleges are either independent or a part of some University.


Admission Process


Admissions test requirements vary from institution to institution. It mostly depends on what course you want to opt for. Most institutions require an English language proficiency test like IELTS or TOEFL. You may need to take other standardized tests, such as GMAT, GRE, and SAT, depending on the program you are applying for.


Visa Application Process


Studying in the USA requires a student visa (F1-Visa). The application documents are to be submitted personally at the US Consulate in your home country. A personal interview is an essential part of the application process.

To obtain a student visa for the United States you are required to submit a financial statement as proof of sufficient funds to cover your stay. You can provide this evidence by submitting a declaration of a surety or a bank statement. Your personal ESC student advisor will provide the required templates.

International students are can work on campus with an F1-Visa. It is possible for you to work in the country for up to one year after you have completed your academic degree.

An F1 visa holder student is permitted to work a part-time on-campus (20 hours per week) but not off campus during the first year of study. After the first year of study, permission to work off campus may be granted by the school in case of curricular work requirements (co-operative educations programs or the ones that require internships or any other work needs necessary for degree completion). DHS/ICE may grant permission for off-campus employment after the first year in cases of demonstrated economic hardship. F1 students are also eligible for 12 months of optional practical training. Students in science, technology, engineering, or mathematics fields may be eligible for up to 29 months of optional practical training. J1 students are eligible for 18 months of academic training (three years if you have completed a doctoral degree).
